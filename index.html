Awards and Acknowledgments

Video Game Design Project - 'Trainhem'

As part of my Bachelor studies I completed a Major Video Games Project in which I worked with a small team as the Lead Vision Holder and a Secondary Programmer to design and build a game named ‘Trainhem’. This involved us developing the game idea which we pitched to industry professionals at SMG Studio to receive feedback before developing the game. This required me to coordinate tasks with people and manage communication within our team to make sure we met deadlines, as well as making design decisions, level design and assist with the programming.

We were given 14 weeks to design and build the game. Trainhem is a top down, 2D, puzzle game that was developed for mobile devices. The goal of each level is to get the train safely from the start to the end by rotating each hexagonal tile piece to line up the tracks. Each level increases in difficulty as more types of trains are introduced which are faster or longer, as well as having multiple trains at one time.

For this project our team was recognised by the University, the Head of the Department of Computing and Unit Convenor for our outstanding results and excellent performance in this unit that has placed us in an elite top performing group.
